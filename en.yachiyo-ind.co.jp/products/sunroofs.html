Sunroofs allow natural light and fresh air into the interior of a vehicle, giving passengers a sense of openness and refreshment.They also add accents and a sense of luxury to the exterior design, increasing the value of the vehicle.
Furthermore, because it is mounted at the highest point in the car, it must be lighter so that it does not affect driving performance or safety performance, even though it is equipped with various mechanisms.
Yachiyo always adheres to the design philosophy of ”lighter” and ”thinner” products, and responds to the needs of our customers by offering everything from standard sunroofs to large-opening panoramic sunroofs.

Product Features

Evolution of Sunroof – Pursuing Light Weight and Thinness

Lightness contributes not only to a car’s driving performance but also to fuel efficiency.
The thinness also contributes to the realization of a spacious interior space.
Yachiyo’s sunroofs have consistently achieved top-class lightness and thinness by using plastic and consolidating, and integrating component parts.

Honda Prelude (1982-1987)

Panoramic Sunroof provides an even more open feeling

A panoramic sunroof that opens up the roof area more than a conventional sunroof.
It is compatible with Mini-Vans and SUVs, and the natural light that reaches close to the rear seats provides a comfortable moving space for all seats.

Achieving both a maximum sense of openness and an attractive exterior
(PANORAMA Flagship: mass production development completed)

The PANORAMA Flagship, which has the open feel of a sunroof over the entire roof, has a luxurious roof design that makes the car even more attractive. 
If the practical application of self-driving technology changes the way we spend time in cars, it has the potential to become natural equipment.
